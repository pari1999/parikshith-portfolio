Enhancing UAV Control with Large Language Models
Draft Under Review

Master thesis case study turning natural-language commands into grounded UAV actions through context-aware planning, Language-Guided Abstraction, and safe deterministic path execution.

  • Designed an intent-to-action architecture linking command parsing, spatial grounding, LGA context filtering, LLM planning, and ROS 2 execution.
  • Validated the system in simulation and a physical Crazyflie 2.1 human-robot interaction study with voice-command tasks.
  • Reduced prompt bloat in dense scenes, held planner latency near ~3.0 s, and improved execution success from 87.7% to 92.2%.
